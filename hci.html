HCI Analysis

Since our application is a bot and a digital assistant - there are very few aspects of this that could be changed as there is not much of a front-end. Hence there was an added emphasis on making the flow of conversation as smooth as possible and as a result we made many mockups to see this flow during the design stage.

As we were aware of the limited front-end options - we focussed on the few ways that were possible to make this much better. We added a help card and welcome card for the user and gave the bot more of a human personality.

HCI Principles

Throughout the development of the project we have paid careful attention to the following HCI design principles to ensure we were building a platform with the customer needs in mind.
HCI Principle Description of application to our project
Visibility The only visual part of the project is the bot - which has the same UI as any chat platoform. To address this point, we have made cards to aid the user and to convey the information on screen to them in a much more digestable and appealing form. Any buttons to take them out of the chat are clear and are only shown on the cards when user has gotten the intended outcome from their conversation - e.g. once a class is ready to be booked
Affordance As this is a bot application, we have made sure the user will know how to interact with it to get their intended outcome. We offer a welcome card at the start that will give them some key commands and also a help card upon request to help them navigate the app. The buttons are clearly named so the user knows when to click them and what it would do.
Feedback The feedback from both the bot and digital assistant is fast enough as and within a reasonable time frame when dealing with requests. The feedback is also usually well-directed and targetted at the user intent with a good match to what their intended outcome.
Mapping The only controls in this project are the user intents - whether typed of given orally. The user intent will map to a specific action decided by our natural-language processing and what intent is determined. The outcome will be a response from the bot or digital assistant.
Consistency The design and responses from the bot and digital assistant mean they are all fairly consistent with one another. There is an overall theme to the way the intents are handled and the responses given - meaning the user will not be surprised by a response and will know what to expect. The styling of the cards has also been kept consistent.
Constraints The natural language processing is not perfect hence not all intents may be found however we find a the correct intents in a reasonable number of cases within error. Also the bot platform is fairly new - hence certain more complex features may be more difficult to implement.
